About Learn to Rise

A small, thoughtful practice built on twenty years of experience, academic research, and a genuine understanding of what it means to find learning difficult.

MPhil (Cambridge), Educational Leadership BSc (Hons) Mathematics PGCE (Further Education) Qualified Teacher Status (QTS) Advanced Skills Teacher (AST)

Over Twenty Years in Mathematics Education

My career began as a college mathematics lecturer. Over twenty years, I taught across the full ability range in schools, working with students of every background and confidence level. I completed an MPhil in Educational Leadership and School Improvement at the University of Cambridge, researching how learning environments and relationships influence student outcomes, particularly for struggling learners.

Looking Beyond Grades

Throughout my career, I saw students who achieved their grades but sometimes carried anxiety and low confidence with them long after the exams were over. That experience shaped what Learn to Rise has become. The approach here addresses how a student thinks and feels about maths, not just what they can produce on an exam paper.

A Personal Understanding of Learning Challenges

I have lived experience with a diagnosed mild developmental language difference (DLD). I also served as a full-time carer for a parent, developing patience and adaptability that are now central to my professional practice. These experiences give me a genuine, personal understanding of what it means to face challenges in learning and in life.

A Small Practice, by Design

Learn to Rise is an intentionally limited one-to-one online practice. This allows me to give focused attention and careful planning to each learner, rather than spreading myself thin. Every student gets the time and thought they deserve.
